A-Z Glossary

Quick definitions for accounting and tax terms

Use this glossary to understand common terms you may see in your returns, accounts, and business finance reports.

A - Allowance: A deductible amount that can reduce taxable income.
B - Bookkeeping: Recording daily financial transactions accurately.
C - Corporation Tax: Tax paid by limited companies on profits.
D - Dividend: Profit paid to shareholders after tax obligations.
E - Expenses: Business costs that may qualify for tax relief.
F - Fiscal Year: A 12-month period used for accounting and reporting.
G - Gross Profit: Revenue minus direct cost of goods or services.
H - HMRC: UK authority responsible for tax collection and customs.
I - Income Tax: Tax charged on individual earnings above allowances.
J - Journal Entry: A formal accounting record of a transaction.
K - KPI: Key performance indicator used to monitor performance.
L - Liability: Money owed by a business, such as loans or tax.
M - Management Accounts: Internal reports prepared periodically for decisions.
N - National Insurance: UK contribution for state benefits and pensions.
O - Overheads: Ongoing operating costs not tied to one sale.
P - PAYE: Pay As You Earn payroll tax deduction system.
Q - Quarter: A three-month reporting period.
R - RTI: Real Time Information payroll submissions to HMRC.
S - Self-Assessment: Personal tax return process for qualifying individuals.
T - Turnover: Total sales income before costs are deducted.
U - UTR: Unique Taxpayer Reference issued by HMRC.
V - VAT: Value Added Tax charged on eligible goods and services.
W - Working Capital: Funds available for day-to-day operations.
X - Xero: Popular cloud accounting software used by small businesses.
Y - Year-End: The final date of a company financial period.
Z - Zero-Rated: VAT category taxed at 0% but still reportable.
